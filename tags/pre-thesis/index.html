Context-Essay

Oct 14, 2022

Introduction

My research question will be around how to improve design collaboration with Augmented Reality. I want to present a case that non-expert clients and designers can work together for interior design at the same time and space, with the computer program of AR device joining and helping the discussion and evolve of design project simultaneously. For example, the on-site discussion and communication of interior design will involve gesture, hand pointing, verbal and eye communication, and if we can have computer recognize and process the natural input to generate design plans, and realize as virtual models and present to users as holograms, it would be of great help for the communication. By user experiments with the prototype I want to find out how will the interaction it proposed between human and computer, human and human will enforce the collaboration in design, and how it will empower the non-experts in design activity by helping them become more aware of their desired outcomes and how to precisely express in the process of interacting with the system and the designer.

Related Works

Augmented Reality in Design Collaboration

There currently exists several studies of collaborating on tasks in Augmented reality, and the importance of the communication and visibility of other participants is recognized[1][2][3][4]. Billinghurst’s study shows how the “shared space” in 3D computer supported collaborative work[1]. Their experiment is for two players to play the game of moving virtual objects under various experimental conditions and with different roles, and the analyze of the result supports that the performance of users in the game can be significantly improved if they can communicate in body language, and stressing the importance of the sense of existence of collaborator and word of team-working in virtual space. Also, the virtual environment is considered interesting by participants. Except for this compare of interaction circumstance, there are also research and projects focusing on the task of design, which is strongly related to my question and requires more engagement for users. Remiko and Jun presented a hand-held augmented reality system for collaborative design early in 1996[2]. By holding a display, the users can see the virtual object in real space through the screen. It supports significantly 100 users in the same time, but the disadvantage is also obvious, only one user can have the ownership to move the object, and the eye and gesture communication among users is limited by the hand held display. Then in 1999[3], Kiyikawa et al presented a system for sharing seamlessly integrated VR and AR experience in design. Users can communicate with others in AR and switch to VR for scaling virtual objects or changing arbitrary perspective. The study of its experiments shows that being able to obstruct each others physically makes it preferable in AR in the aspect of task completing time and subjective evaluation. This system did not support some movement and sharing the view of virtual object that larger than a desk size. More close to the case my proposal is considering, Wang’s study[4] presented a mixed reality scenario for designers and customers to collaborate. With the computer-meditated telepresence system, it tried to extend the boundary of mixed reality system, providing the circumstance that designer’s work can be shared and managed while collaborating. The system it presents to me is unnecessarily complicate without integrating the sensors and applying too much markers, but it provided a detailed analysis of boundaries, and explored the potentials of distributed collaborative design.
