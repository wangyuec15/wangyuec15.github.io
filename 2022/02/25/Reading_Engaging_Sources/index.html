Reading - Engaging Sources

Feb 25, 2022

Summary:

This article tells about how to make the best use of secondary source for research. Here are some guidance of what should we take, from a source, for literature review and building up our research assumption.

  • record all the bibliographical information of an important source, such as: title, author, publication institute, date, page, ISBN or URL and so on.
  • read the sources twice. In the first time, try to find out your interest point, confusing point and how does it makes sense. For the second time, think about how you would respond to its challenge to your point. Try summarize the source. Avoid blindly trusting authority’s work.
  • read the source deliberately to find a problem.
    • if you feel agree with a source claim, consider how to upper additional support, how to prove its assumes or speculates, and how to apply a claim more widely.
    • for disagreement, locate it at part-whole contradictions, developmental or historical contradictions, external cause-effect contradictions, or contradictions of perspective.
  • reading for arguments
    • be aware of how sources can contradict to your argument, for responding them to complete your argument.
    • find the pattern to follow in the sources to make your own argument
  • read for data to use as evidence
  • read for claims to use as support

Taking notes

  • Paper-based notes and index cards, computer word processor, application and reference management system are all useful tools for taking notes.
  • Summarize when you need only the point of a passage, section, or even whole article or book. Use quote, paraphrase or summarize base on what you need from a source

get the context to accurately capture the source’s meaning.

Reflection:

I think there are two main idea that the author conveys by the methodology of reading secondary source: take out the basic important information of a literature, reading with thinking to support my argument and step further from existing research. I found the part of taking note out-dated, for we now have a lot more fancy tools for source management. The point I agree most is that respond to readers’ predictable questions and disagreements. It will be a failure if our research is unconvincing.

Question:

For research relating to computer science, I found that sometimes a good research is more from a creative design or algorithm than reflection on previous works, do you agree sometimes we should also make some attempts to get our hand dirty before wrap up the literature review?
