Reading - Cultivating the Critical Imagination

Feb 04, 2022

The article takes our CodeLab as research objective and example, to reveal how the “design studio” can cultivate the critical imagination of learners in the cross-discipline field of STS and design.

This paper focuses on the CoDeLab not only in terms of this specific location, but also its cultural environment - the curriculum, the composition of the faculty and students, the different research topics and methods, etc. It also emphasizes the research projects should be “inquiry-driven rather than simply shaped by disciplinary inclinations, industry imperatives, or methodological comfort zones”(P7). Students learn to situate technologies in specific historical and socio-material contexts, critically consider the implications of their design, deployment, and use, and reflexively harness these understandings in processes of creative design. The idea of the studio combines ‘learning by doing’ with the university’s post-disciplinary atmosphere. This article also presents several research of students, to show their variety attitude towards technology, scales of intervention, and sociotechnical understandings of design.

Question:
In this article, program diversity is revealed to have beneficial to strengthen the faith that “not construe technologies as solutions but sought instead to investigate them critically”, for keeping a a critical distance from a dominant image of technology(p17). Besides the external environment, what do you think is other way we can actively take to avoid having technology as solutions in research?
